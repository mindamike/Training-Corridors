## Preamble
These seven axioms constitute the foundational layer of the Recursive Substrate Theory (RST), the internal project name for the framework whose mathematical core is published as the Viability Corridor Framework (VCF). The axioms were developed prior to the corridor derivations and motivated the specific mathematical formalization that followed.

The axioms are substrate-neutral and domain-independent. They make no reference to specific physical systems, equations, or parameter values. Their function is to specify the minimal structural commitments from which the VCF derivations follow: that organized systems have hierarchical structure, that two conjugate operations generate that structure, that each layer has finite capacity, and that capacity exceedance resolves in one of two qualitatively distinct ways.

The two resolution modes of Axiom 5 — emergent reconfiguration (continuous, coherence-preserving) and structural collapse (discontinuous, coherence-breaking) — correspond exactly to the transcritical (TC) and saddle-node (SN) bifurcation classes in the VCF normal form analysis. The 2:1 exponent ratio, the directed admissibility theorem, the global alternation of boundary types, and the cosmological parameter derivations all follow from the mathematical formalization of this dichotomy under Kramers escape theory applied to the two normal forms.

These axioms are published here as a timestamped record of the foundational commitments that preceded and motivated the VCF mathematical development, which can be read alongside the VCF technical documents at DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19794956.

## The Seven Axioms

### Axiom 0: Recursive Substrate
Physical reality consists of hierarchically nested layers generated by recursive application of operations on a unified substrate. Each layer is characterized by distinct structural pathways — the accessible configurations and transition routes within phase space at that scale.

### Axiom 1: Substrate and Operations
At the deepest accessible layer, the substrate is energy. Two conjugate operations act on this substrate:
* **Cohere**: localization of energy into bound states, symmetry breaking, reduction of accessible phase space.
* **Persist**: expansion of correlations, symmetry preservation, propagation across available degrees of freedom.

### Axiom 2: Initial Anisotropy
Observable structure originates from an initial asymmetry in the cohere–persist balance, whether intrinsic to the substrate or imposed by boundary conditions. This anisotropy establishes preferred structural pathways at the earliest accessible scales.

### Axiom 3: Layer Generation
Each application of cohere–persist at layer N generates distinguishable structure through established structural pathways, which becomes the substrate for layer N+1. Layers are defined by characteristic energy, length, and time scales and by the topology of available phase space at that scale.

### Axiom 4: Finite Capacity
Each layer's structural pathways have finite capacity to absorb throughput (energy and entropy flux) while maintaining coherent dynamics. Exceeding this capacity saturates available pathways, forcing resolution at adjacent layers.

### Axiom 5: Resolution Dichotomy
When structural pathway capacity is exceeded, resolution occurs through one of exactly two modes:
* **Emergent reconfiguration**: formation of new structural pathways at the same or higher layer, preserving coherence. Continuous character. Corresponds to the transcritical (TC) bifurcation class in the VCF normal form analysis.
* **Structural collapse**: existing pathways fail, coherence is lost, throughput transfers to adjacent layers. Discontinuous character. Corresponds to the saddle-node (SN) bifurcation class in the VCF normal form analysis.

This dichotomy is the foundational source of the 2:1 asymmetry in VCF noise-scaling exponents (α_TC = 1/3, α_SN = 2/3) and of the alternating TC–SN boundary structure across the organizational hierarchy.

### Axiom 6: Causality
The cohere–persist dynamic is causal and propagates through available structural pathways. Quantum entanglement represents persistent coherence across degrees of freedom where structural pathways have not yet differentiated into independent localized states.
